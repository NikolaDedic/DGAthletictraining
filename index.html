IN-SEASON TRAINING

We find that a lot of basketball/sport related injuries stem from:
1) poor postural strength
2) incorrect bio mechanical patters or how we move. With in-season training we begin with balance and or joint integrity to build a strong foundation first, through which we lessen the risk of injury. Once we have that in place and athletes joints feel strong and move in a safe, economic way we will then move on to functional training. This is where we focus on making the athlete strong to withstand the physical demands specific to their sport. Making athletes physically smart and NOT prone to injuries is one of our MOST IMPORTANT goals. We sporadically intertwine sport specific strength exercises with general strength and power development. We find that it is important to train maximal strength and sport specific power at least once a week in-season.

OFF-SEASON TRAINING

BSAPProgram is our summer program designed for athletes that want to work on their weaknesses and work on their athleticism. BSAP is a month long program and it stands for Basketball Speed, Agility and Power. BSAP Program integrates 2 sport performance training components.
•Power developing component designed to increase explosive speed, speed strength, quickness, first step, vertical ability, powerful reactivity and sprinting speed. In this phase of the program we work on building a more advanced level of sport conditioning to be able to transfer it effectively and safely into sport movement.
•Speed and Agility component Carefully periodized and progressed sport performance, sport movement and plyometric drills (agility, speed and reactivity). In this phase we concentrate on developing movement patterns that are specific to basketball. Following the movement cognitive phase we shift focus to executing the movements under high velocity in a dynamic setting. Increasing the athletes’ vertical ability is one of the skills we work on here.
